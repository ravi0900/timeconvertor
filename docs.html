Guide to Epoch Time & ISO 8601

This page provides a guide to the time formats used in our converters and how the conversion process works.

What is Epoch Time?

Epoch time, also known as Unix time or POSIX time, is a system for describing a point in time. It is the number of seconds that have elapsed since the Unix epoch, which was at 00:00:00 UTC on 1 January 1970.

Because computer systems often require higher precision, Epoch time is frequently represented in smaller units:

  • Seconds: The standard unit (e.g., 1723948799).
  • Milliseconds (ms): Seconds multiplied by 1,000. This is commonly used in web technologies like JavaScript.
  • Microseconds (¬µs): Seconds multiplied by 1,000,000.
  • Nanoseconds (ns): Seconds multiplied by 1,000,000,000. Used in high-precision systems.

Epoch time is useful because it is a simple, unambiguous number that is independent of time zones, making it ideal for computer systems to store and manipulate timestamps.

What is ISO 8601?

ISO 8601 is an international standard for representing dates and times. Its goal is to provide a well-defined, unambiguous method of representing date and time information to avoid misinterpretation, especially when data is transferred between countries with different conventions.

A common ISO 8601 format looks like this: YYYY-MM-DDTHH:mm:ss.sssZ

  • YYYY-MM-DD: The date (year, month, day).
  • T: A separator indicating that the time part follows.
  • HH:mm:ss.sss: The time (hours, minutes, seconds, and milliseconds).
  • Z: The time zone designator. 'Z' stands for Zulu time, which is equivalent to UTC. It can also be a positive or negative offset, like +05:30 or -07:00.

This format is widely used in modern APIs and databases because it is machine-readable and contains complete information about the date, time, and timezone in a single string.

How the Conversions Work

From Human Date to Epoch Timestamp

This conversion calculates the total number of seconds elapsed from the Unix epoch (00:00:00 UTC on Jan 1, 1970) to the target date. The logic is to sum up the seconds for every day, hour, minute, and second that has passed, while correctly accounting for leap years.

Example: Convert the UTC date 2025-08-18 10:30:00.

The total time elapsed is broken down:

  • Seconds from Jan 1, 1970 to Dec 31, 2024 (55 years, including 14 leap years).
  • Seconds from Jan 1, 2025 to Aug 17, 2025 (229 days).
  • Seconds for the time on Aug 18, 2025 (10 hours, 30 minutes).

Summing these up gives the final epoch timestamp in seconds: 1755586600.

From this, we can derive higher precision values:

  • Milliseconds: 1755586600000 (seconds √ó 1,000)
  • Microseconds: 1755586600000000 (seconds √ó 1,000,000)
  • Nanoseconds: 1755586600000000000 (seconds √ó 1,000,000,000)

From Epoch Timestamp to Human Date

This is the reverse process. The total seconds are divided by the number of seconds in a day (86,400) to find the number of full days that have passed and the remaining seconds for the time of day.

Example: Convert the timestamp 1755586600 (in seconds).

  1. Calculate days and remaining seconds:
    • 1755586600 √∑ 86400 = 20319.289... This means 20,319 full days have passed.
    • 1755586600 % 86400 = 37,800 remaining seconds for the time of day.
  2. Determine the date: Starting from Jan 1, 1970, adding 20,319 days (accounting for leap years) brings us to the date August 18, 2025.
  3. Determine the time from remaining seconds:
    • Hours: 37800 √∑ 3600 = 10.5, so 10 hours.
    • Minutes: The remaining 0.5 hours is 0.5 √ó 60 = 30 minutes.
    • Seconds: 0 seconds.

The resulting UTC date is Mon, 18 Aug 2025 10:30:00 GMT.

Handling ISO 8601 and Timezones

The ISO 8601 format includes timezone information, which must be handled to get an accurate UTC time before converting to an Epoch timestamp.

Example: Convert the ISO string 2025-08-18T16:00:00+05:30.

  1. Identify the components: The date is 2025-08-18, the time is 16:00:00, and the timezone is +05:30 (5 hours and 30 minutes ahead of UTC).
  2. Normalize to UTC: To find the equivalent UTC time, we subtract the timezone offset from the given time.
  3. Calculation: 16:00:00 - 05:30 = 10:30:00. The date remains the same.
  4. Resulting UTC Time: The equivalent time in UTC is 2025-08-18 10:30:00 UTC.

Once the date is in UTC, it can be converted to an Epoch timestamp as explained in the first section, resulting in 1755586600 seconds.
